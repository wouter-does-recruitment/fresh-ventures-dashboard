Key Recommendations — 2026 Cohort

Based on full analysis of the 2025–2026 Co-founder Venture Building Program: outreach data (20,732 leads), 50+ CV profiles, ClickUp pipeline data, and the end-to-end AI/automation flow. These recommendations are prioritised by expected impact on the next cohort.

Funnel & Application
Biggest drop-off opportunities
🚨

Add a lightweight pre-screen before Typeform + Hireflix High impact

The application form is the single biggest drop-off point: 26% of applicants who started never finished, and 18 strong outbound profiles (including a FallingWalls 2020 winner and a PhD plant biologist with 20+ years agronomy experience) stalled here. Typeform + Hireflix together is a heavy ask for a cold-to-warm lead. A 3-question pre-screen — mission alignment, founding motivation, availability — before the full flow would reduce friction and recover 2–4 strong candidates per cohort.

Source: Outbound profile analysis · 18 stalled at application stage · 10/18 had founding experience
📋

Run exit surveys for dropouts High impact

7 of 20 dropouts gave no reason at all. A simple automated trigger at the point of status change ("Dropped out") — a 2-question survey via n8n — would surface patterns over time. The current data shows misunderstanding of the program (co-founding vs hiring) as a recurring theme: Katerina Joannides explicitly thought Fresh was hiring, not co-founding. Clearer messaging earlier in the funnel could prevent this.

Source: ClickUp comment analysis · 20 dropouts · 7 no reason given
📝

Clarify "co-founding" vs "hiring" in early messaging Medium impact

Multiple candidates dropped citing misaligned expectations — they thought they were applying for a job, not committing to a co-founding journey. Adding one clear line to the outreach sequence and the application form header ("You'll enter as an individual and potentially leave as a co-founder of a new venture") would filter this earlier and save review time.

Source: Drop reason analysis · "Thought we were hiring a co-founder" — Katerina Joannides
Outreach Strategy
Targeting & campaign optimisation
📡

Double down on warm retargeting campaigns High impact

The "LI reactors & event attendees" campaign (people who already engaged with Fresh content) produced 3 of the 5 confirmed outreach hires and by far the highest reply volume (2,978). Cold outreach campaigns like "Innovation in Farming and Food" (12,558 leads, primarily corporate Product Managers) showed far weaker conversion. For the next cohort: allocate more budget to warm audiences — event attendees, LinkedIn post reactors, alumni networks — and reduce cold volume.

Source: Campaign analysis · LI reactors = 3 hires · Innovation campaign = 0 confirmed hires
🎯

Sharpen targeting criteria: prioritise food/ag + founding experience High impact

CV analysis of all 50 outbound applicants shows a clear pattern: candidates who converted had food/ag background + senior experience + founding signals in their profile. The "Innovation" campaign targeted corporate Product Managers and R&D Managers — strong on tech but weak on food system connection and entrepreneurial drive. Boolean searches and Clay enrichment should add "founder", "co-founder", "startup" as positive signals and deprioritise pure corporate titles.

Source: Outbound profile analysis · 72% Masters+ · food score avg 2.9 in application stage vs 1.6 in med/low potential
🌍

Expand sourcing into underrepresented regions Medium impact

Current outreach is heavily Western Europe focused — NL + UK alone account for ~13% of all outreach. High-potential markets that are underrepresented: Nordics (Norway, Finland), CEE (Czech Republic, Romania), and LATAM (Brazil, Mexico). These regions have strong agri-food innovation ecosystems and could diversify the co-founder pool meaningfully for future cohorts.

Source: Geography analysis · 18 identified countries · NL + UK = 2,103 of 15,803 outbound leads
AI & Automation
Flow optimisation for next cohort
🤖

Add human touchpoint for top 10–15% of outbound leads High impact

The AI follow-up sequence works well at volume but fails at the individual level for high-potential profiles. Humberto Martinez (FallingWalls winner, AgTech CEO) and Noël Magnin (PhD, Ecorobotix) both dropped at the application form — a short personal message from Huub or Bart at the right moment could have converted either of them. Recommendation: flag the top 10–15% of positive replies for a manual personalised follow-up before the application CTA.

Source: AI component analysis · Outbound profile analysis · Humberto Martinez, Noël Magnin
🔍

Review "Unknown" and "Polite Decline" reply buckets manually Medium impact

472 replies were categorised as "Unknown/Unclear" and 396 as "Polite Decline" — together nearly 900 conversations that may contain hidden positives misrouted by the AI. A one-time manual review of these buckets, or a re-run through a refined categorisation prompt, could surface 20–50 candidates worth re-engaging for the next cohort.

Source: HeyReach reply analysis · 5,684 total replies · 472 unknown + 396 polite decline
⚙️

Implement cross-mission deduplication Low impact

Currently there is no cross-mission deduplication — leads may receive multiple outreach sequences from different senders (e.g. Huub and Bart both approaching the same person for different missions). While this was a deliberate choice, it risks alienating high-value prospects. A simple dedup check in Clay before syncing to HeyReach would prevent this for the highest-priority leads.

Source: Process documentation · No cross-mission dedup currently active
Late Stage & Offer
Converting offers to hires
🤝

Conduct structured exit conversations with offer declines Medium impact

9 candidates declined after receiving an offer — a 31% offer decline rate. Notably, offer-declined candidates had stronger founding experience (67%) than the hires themselves (40%), suggesting these were high-quality profiles lost for non-profile reasons. A short structured call at the point of decline ("What would have made this a yes?") would build a feedback loop for improving the offer stage. Key themes to probe: program intensity, financial support, co-founder matching confidence.

Source: Pipeline analysis · 9 offer declines · offer-declined founding exp 67% vs hired 40%
